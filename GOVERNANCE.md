# Raven AI Governance Framework

## Vision

Raven AI is your intelligence layer — a sovereign, local-first AI orchestration
platform that puts you in control of your data and compute. Our governance
framework ensures transparency, accountability, and community-driven evolution.

## Core Principles

1. **Sovereignty First** — Users own their data and control their compute
2. **Zero Trust** — Every access is verified, every action is audited
3. **Open by Default** — Transparent development, open source, community driven
4. **Privacy Preserving** — Local-first architecture with federated capabilities
5. **Enterprise Grade** — Security, compliance, and reliability from day one

## Governance Structure

### Executive Steering Committee (ESC)

The ESC provides strategic direction and oversees major project decisions.

* **Barry Clerjuste** — Project Lead, Architecture & Security
* *Community representatives* — (To be elected as community grows)

### Technical Steering Committee (TSC)

The TSC governs technical decisions, architecture, and standards.

* Core architecture decisions
* API and protocol standards
* Security and compliance review
* Release management

### Community Governance

* **RFC Process** — Major changes require a public RFC
* **Lazy Consensus** — Default to approval unless objections raised
* **Code Review** — All changes require review from at least one maintainer
* **Decision Records** — All architectural decisions documented in ADRs

## Decision Making

* **Consensus Seeking** — Strive for community consensus
* **Voting** — If consensus cannot be reached, TSC votes (simple majority)
* **Veto** — Security-related decisions can be vetoed by the Project Lead

## Compliance & Ethics

* All contributors must adhere to the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
* Security vulnerabilities handled per [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)
* Data privacy and ethical AI practices are core requirements

## Amendment Process

This governance document may be amended by:
1. RFC submission describing the proposed change
2. 2-week comment period for community feedback
3. TSC vote with supermajority (⅔) approval
